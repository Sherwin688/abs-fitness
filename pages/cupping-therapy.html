Cupping Therapy Service
Service Description:
Cupping Therapy is a gentle treatment where special cups are placed on your skin. These cups create a nice, relaxed feeling, and they can help you feel better
Who is it for?:
  • If you want a soothing and peaceful experience
  • It's okay for everyone, no matter how old you are or if you exercise
  • Good for people who have muscle pain, stress, or feel tired
Service Benefits:
  • It helps you relax and eases muscle tension
  • A comfortable and calm place for your session
  • It boosts your blood flow and reduces stress
  • It's a simple and natural way to feel good
  • Gentle cupping to help you relax and feel better
Types:

Myo-Kinetic Cupping
is a specialized form of cupping therapy that focuses on releasing muscle tension and promoting flexibility. During a Myo-Kinetic cupping session, suction cups are strategically applied to specific muscle areas to help relax tight muscles, improve blood flow, and enhance overall mobility. This therapy is often sought after by athletes and individuals with muscular discomfort to help them recover and maintain peak physical performance.

Static Cupping
involves placing suction cups on specific areas of the body to create a gentle vacuum effect. This technique is primarily used to relieve muscle tension, increase blood flow, and promote relaxation.

Dynamic Cupping
involves the use of suction cups that glide across the skin's surface. This technique helps stimulate circulation, release muscle knots, and improve flexibility. It's particularly effective for targeting larger muscle groups and promoting overall muscle health.

Dry Cupping
is a traditional form of cupping therapy where suction cups are placed on the skin to create a vacuum effect. This therapy is often used to alleviate muscle tension, improve blood flow, and promote relaxation. It's a non-invasive method that can provide relief for various health concerns

Wet Cupping / Hijama
is a form of cupping therapy that involves creating small incisions on the skin after dry cupping and then applying suction cups to draw out a small amount of blood. It is believed to help remove toxins from the body and promote overall well-being. Wet cupping should be performed by trained professionals in a sterile environment to ensure safety and hygiene.

Fire Cupping
uses heated glass or bamboo cups to create a vacuum when applied to the skin. The cups are heated with an open flame before being placed on specific areas of the body. As the air inside the cup cools, it creates a suction effect, which can help with muscle tension, blood flow, and relaxation.

Dry Needling
Dry needling uses tiny needles in muscle knots to help with pain and movement. Trained experts safely ease muscle issues. It's good for different body problems, like pain and stiffness.
